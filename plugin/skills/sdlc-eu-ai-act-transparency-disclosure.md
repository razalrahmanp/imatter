## Rule

Users interacting with AI systems must know they're interacting with AI. AI-generated content must be labeled. Emotion-recognition and biometric-categorization systems must inform users. These are the EU AI Act transparency obligations under Article 50.

## Three categories

### 1. AI interaction disclosure

When a user interacts with an AI system (chatbot, voice assistant, AI-driven UI), they must be informed — unless it's obvious from context.

```tsx
function ChatWindow() {
  return (
    <div>
      <header>
        <h2>Support Chat</h2>
        <p className="text-muted">
          You're chatting with an AI assistant. For complex issues, ask to speak to a human.
        </p>
      </header>
      ...
    </div>
  );
}
```

Disclosure must be clear and at the start of the interaction. Hidden in fine print = not compliant.

### 2. Emotion recognition / biometric categorization

If your system infers emotion from voice/face/text, or categorizes users biometrically (age, ethnicity, etc.), tell them:

```
This call may use voice-tone analysis to route your request.
You can opt out by saying "no analysis."
```

Some uses are restricted entirely (real-time biometric ID in public spaces by law enforcement, with narrow exceptions).

### 3. Synthetic / deepfake content

AI-generated or manipulated content (text, image, audio, video) must be labeled — especially when it could be mistaken for authentic:

- Deepfakes: visible label or metadata watermark
- AI-written articles: byline or footer ("Generated with AI assistance")
- Synthetic images: caption or embedded provenance

Exception: clearly artistic / satirical content where the labeling would defeat the purpose, but with sensible limits.

## Implementation — design patterns

### Chatbot opening message

```
Hi! I'm an AI assistant from Acme Support. I can answer common questions and help with basic tasks. For anything urgent or complex, type "human" to be transferred.
```

This serves three purposes:
- Discloses AI (compliance)
- Sets expectations
- Provides an escape to a human (good UX, and supports human-oversight obligation for high-risk systems)

### Synthetic content watermark

For AI-generated images/video: embed metadata (C2PA Content Credentials, IPTC standards), display a visible badge/label, and ideally a perceptual watermark.

For AI-generated text: visible footer or byline ("This summary was generated by AI").

### Voice / call disclosure

```
"This call may be recorded and processed by AI for quality assurance.
Press 0 at any time to be connected to a human agent."
```

Spoken at the start of the call. The "press 0" gives an opt-out.

## What is NOT enough

| Insufficient | Problem |
|---|---|
| Buried in privacy policy | Not "clear and distinguishable" |
| Disclosure after the interaction | Too late |
| One-time consent at signup | Must inform per-interaction |
| Tiny watermark in corner of deepfake | Should be visible / detectable |
| "AI may be involved" vague language | Must clearly identify AI involvement |

## Special case — companion / persona AI

If your AI presents as a persona (named character, "Aria from Customer Support"), the disclosure must still happen even though the persona is named. Don't let the persona effectively conceal that it's AI.

## Children

For services accessed by children: simpler language; consider gating the AI features behind parent consent.

## Anti-patterns

- ❌ No AI disclosure on a chatbot ("just looks like chat")
- ❌ AI-generated articles published with human bylines
- ❌ Deepfake content labeled only in metadata, not visibly
- ❌ "Acme Assistant" persona that never says "I'm AI"
- ❌ Disclosure only in T&Cs (not at point of interaction)
- ❌ Emotion-recognition systems analyzing calls without notice
- ❌ Synthetic voice in IVR sounding human without disclosure

## Gate criteria

- Every AI-powered user-facing feature has a disclosure at the point of interaction
- Disclosure is visible without scrolling / hunting
- AI-generated content (images, video, text) is labeled per its type
- Emotion-recognition or biometric-categorization features have informed-consent flow
- A QA pass verifies disclosure across all AI-touched flows
- Disclosure text is localized for each EU language the product supports
